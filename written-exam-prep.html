W1Quiz
Write down all joint movements for Parivrtta Janu Sirsasana (Revolved Head-to-Knee Pose).
Answer — Joint Movements: · Spine — Flexion & Rotation
· Hip (bent leg) — Flexion
· Knee (bent leg) — Flexion
· Knee (straight leg) — Extension
· Foot (bent leg, lower) — Dorsiflexion
· Foot (straight leg) — Plantar Flexion
· Shoulder (upper arm) — Flexion & Abduction
· Shoulder (lower arm) — Flexion & Rotation
· Elbow (lower arm) — Flexion (pressed into floor)
· Elbow (upper arm) — Flexion (bend toward ceiling)
· Neck — Flexion & Rotation
Key cues: Lift hip point · Slightly raise sitting bone · Lengthen spine to rotate · Press shin into floor · Bend upper elbow toward ceiling · Press lower elbow into floor

Yoga Philosophy

योग दर्शन 10 questions
Q1Teacher
The word "Yoga" is derived from the Sanskrit root "Yuj" which means:
  • A.To stretch
  • B.To unite or join✓
  • C.To breathe
  • D.To meditate
"Yuj" = to unite or connect — union of body, mind, and consciousness with universal reality.
Not stretching — that is a modern Western oversimplification.
Q2Teacher
According to Patanjali's Yoga Sutras, how many limbs (branches) are there in Ashtanga Yoga?
  • A.6
  • B.8✓
  • C.7
  • D.9
The 8 limbs: Yama · Niyama · Asana · Pranayama · Pratyahara · Dharana · Dhyana · Samadhi
Do not confuse Patanjali's Ashtanga (8 limbs philosophy) with modern Ashtanga Vinyasa physical practice.
Q3Teacher
Which of these is NOT one of the five Yamas?
  • A.Ahimsa
  • B.Satya
  • C.Dharana✓
  • D.Asteya
5 Yamas: Ahimsa · Satya · Asteya · Brahmacharya · Aparigraha. Dharana = concentration (6th limb).
Dharana sounds ethical but it is a mental limb — not a Yama.
Q4Teacher
What does "Pranayama" literally mean?
  • A.Breathing exercise
  • B.Life force control✓
  • C.Mental concentration
  • D.Physical posture
"Prana" = vital energy. "Ayama" = expansion/control. Breath is the tool to influence prana — not the definition itself.
"Breathing exercise" is an oversimplification. Pranayama works on the energy body using breath as the vehicle.
Q5Teacher
Which philosophical text is considered the foundational text of Ashtanga yoga?
  • A.Bhagavad Gita
  • B.Hatha Yoga Pradipika
  • C.Yoga Sutras of Patanjali✓
  • D.Upanishads
Yoga Sutras = systematic source of classical yoga, defines the 8 limbs. Bhagavad Gita = broader spiritual philosophy. Hatha Yoga Pradipika = practice-oriented.
Q6Teacher
The concept of "Karma" refers to:
  • A.Destiny
  • B.Action and its consequences✓
  • C.Bad luck
  • D.Future predictions
Karma = action + result (cause and effect). Every action leaves a samskara (mental imprint).
"Karma" is widely misused to mean fate. In yogic philosophy it is strictly about action and consequence.
Q7Teacher
What are the three Gunas in Yogic philosophy?
  • A.Sattva, Rajas, Tamas✓
  • B.Prana, Apana, Samana
  • C.Yama, Niyama, Asana
  • D.Dharma, Karma, Maya
The 3 Gunas are fundamental qualities of nature (Prakriti): Sattva = clarity/balance · Rajas = activity/movement · Tamas = inertia/darkness. Everything is a combination of all three.
Other options mix in Vayus (Prana/Apana/Samana) — a completely different framework.
Q8Teacher
What is the ultimate goal of yoga according to classical texts?
  • A.Physical fitness
  • B.Mental relaxation
  • C.Self-realization (Moksha)✓
  • D.Social connection
Moksha = liberation from suffering and ignorance. Fitness and relaxation are side effects, not the goal.
Modern wellness framing reduces yoga to health benefits. The classical tradition points to liberation.
Q9Teacher
The concept of "Chitta Vritti Nirodha" refers to:
  • A.Physical exercise
  • B.Breathing techniques
  • C.Stilling the fluctuations of the mind✓
  • D.Ethical principles
Chitta = mind · Vritti = modifications/fluctuations · Nirodha = cessation. This IS the core definition of yoga from Patanjali's Yoga Sutras.
Q10Teacher
Which Kosha (layer) is directly associated with the physical body?
  • A.Vijnanamaya kosha
  • B.Annamaya kosha✓
  • C.Pranamaya kosha
  • D.Manomaya kosha
The 5 Koshas: 1. Annamaya (physical) · 2. Pranamaya (energy) · 3. Manomaya (mental) · 4. Vijnanamaya (intellect) · 5. Anandamaya (bliss). "Anna" = food → the body made of matter.
Vijnanamaya sounds scientific but it is the intellect layer. Annamaya = the physical food body.
