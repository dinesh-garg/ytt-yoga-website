Class Sequence

A structured guide for the teacher — follow in order

01
Introduce Self
Welcome students. Share your name, background, and brief teaching philosophy. Create a safe, inclusive atmosphere.
02
Ask About Injuries
Invite students to share any physical injuries, limitations, or areas of discomfort. Acknowledge each one and note modifications you will offer.
03
Ask About Hands-On Adjustment
Ask for consent before offering physical assists. Establish a clear signal (e.g. hand gesture or verbal cue) for students to opt out at any time.
04
Intention of Practice
Invite students to set a personal sankalpa — an intention or dedication for the practice. This could be a quality, a person, or simply the act of showing up.
05
3 Breaths of Awareness
Guide three slow, conscious breaths together. Inhale through the nose, exhale through the nose. Allow the body to arrive fully on the mat.
06
Om × 3
Chant Om three times together. The sound of Om unifies the group, clears the mind, and marks the formal opening of practice.
ॐ ॐ ॐ
07
Opening Mantra
Recite the Vakratunda Mahakaya mantra to invoke Ganesha — remover of obstacles — before beginning the physical practice.
वक्रतुण्ड महाकाय सूर्यकोटि समप्रभ।
निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा॥
Vakratunda Mahakaya Suryakoti Samaprabha
Nirvighnam Kuru Me Deva Sarva Karyeshu Sarvada
O Lord with a curved trunk and a mighty body, whose brilliance equals ten million suns — please make all my works free of obstacles, always.
08
Warm Up Exercises
Guide students through a series of gentle warm-up movements to awaken the spine, shoulders, neck, and hips before the sun salutations.

09
Pranamasana + Sun Salutation Mantra
Come to standing at the top of the mat. Guide students into Pranamasana. Recite or play the 12 Surya Namaskar mantras once through before beginning the rounds.

ॐ सवित्रे नमः · ॐ अर्काय नमः · ॐ भास्कराय नमः

Step 9–10 · Sun Salutations

Surya Namaskar

2–3 Rounds · 24 Positions per Round

One complete round = 24 positions: positions 1–12 with the right leg stepping back, positions 13–24 with the left leg stepping back. Perform 2–3 full rounds. Each position is linked to a mantra.

Positions 1–12 · Right Leg Back
1
Pranamasana
Prayer Pose
↓ Breathe normallyStill · 2 breaths
Palms together at the heart. Exhale. Eyes closed, body still.
✦ Om Mitraya Namaha — salutations to the friend of all
2
Hasta Utthanasana
Raised Arms Pose
↑ Inhale, arms sweep up
Inhale. Sweep arms up, palms together overhead. Gentle backbend.
✦ Om Ravaye Namaha — salutations to the shining one
3
Padahastasana
Hand to Foot Pose
↓ Exhale, hands to floor
Exhale. Fold forward — palms flat beside the feet, abdomen contracted.
✦ Om Suryaya Namaha — salutations to he who induces activity
The back should not bend fully forward. Keep the spine as straight as possible.
4
Ashwa Sanchalanasana
Equestrian Pose · Right Leg Back
Right leg steps back
↑ Inhale, step right back
Inhale. Step right leg back, lower right knee. Lift the chest, look forward.
✦ Om Bhanave Namaha — salutations to he who illumines
5
Parvatasana
Mountain Pose — Inverted V
↓ Exhale, hips highInverted V shape
Exhale. Both legs back, hips rise high — inverted V. Spine long, heels toward the floor.
✦ Om Khagaya Namaha — salutations to he who moves quickly in the sky
Cautions for inverted postures apply. People with back conditions should seek guidance.
6
Ashtanga Namaskara
Eight-Limbed Salutation
Breath held out — no respiration
Breath held out. Lower knees, chest, chin to mat — eight points of contact. Hips lifted.
✦ Om Pushne Namaha — salutations to the giver of strength
People with serious back problems, high blood pressure or heart conditions should not do this practice.
7
Bhujangasana
Cobra Pose
↑ Inhale, raise torso and arch
Inhale. Slide chest forward and up, arms extend. Shoulders back and down.
✦ Om Hiranya Garbhaya Namaha — salutations to the golden cosmic self
Not advised for people suffering from peptic ulcer, hernia, intestinal tuberculosis or hyperthyroidism.
8
Parvatasana
Mountain Pose — Inverted V
↓ Exhale, raise buttocks
Exhale. Tuck toes, raise hips high — back to inverted V. Spine long.
✦ Om Marichaye Namaha — salutations to the Lord of the Dawn
9
Ashwa Sanchalanasana
Equestrian Pose · Right Leg Forward
Right foot steps forward
↑ Inhale, right foot forward
Inhale. Step right foot forward between the hands. Lift chest, look forward.
✦ Om Adityaya Namaha — salutations to the son of Aditi, the cosmic Mother
The full stretch is not advised for people with knee or ankle problems.
10
Padahastasana
Hand to Foot Pose
↓ Exhale, fold forward
Exhale. Step left foot forward. Fold — palms flat beside the feet.
✦ Om Savitre Namaha — salutations to the Lord of Creation
11
Hasta Utthanasana
Raised Arms Pose
↑ Inhale, sweep arms up
Inhale. Rise up, arms sweep overhead. Gentle backbend.
✦ Om Arkaya Namaha — salutations to he who is fit to be praised
12
Pranamasana
Prayer Pose · End of Side 1
↓ Exhale, return to prayerPause · 1 breath
Exhale. Palms together at the heart. Pause one breath before the left side.
✦ Om Bhaskaraya Namaha — salutations to he who leads to enlightenment
Positions 13–24 · Left Leg Back
13
Pranamasana
Prayer Pose
Breathe normally
Palms at the heart. Breathe normally. Pause — feel the warmth before the second side.
✦ Om Mitraya Namaha
14
Hasta Utthanasana
Raised Arms Pose
↑ Inhale, arms up
Inhale. Arms sweep up overhead. Gentle backbend.
✦ Om Ravaye Namaha
15
Padahastasana
Hand to Foot Pose
↓ Exhale, hands to floor
Exhale. Fold forward — palms flat beside the feet.
✦ Om Suryaya Namaha
16
Ashwa Sanchalanasana
Equestrian Pose · Left Leg Back
Left leg steps back
↑ Inhale, step left back
Inhale. Step left leg back, lower left knee. Lift chest, look forward.
✦ Om Bhanave Namaha
17
Parvatasana
Mountain Pose — Inverted V
↓ Exhale, hips high
Exhale. Both legs back, hips rise — inverted V.
✦ Om Khagaya Namaha
18
Ashtanga Namaskara
Eight-Limbed Salutation
Breath held out — no respiration
Breath held out. Knees, chest, chin to mat — eight-point contact.
✦ Om Pushne Namaha
19
Bhujangasana
Cobra Pose
↑ Inhale, raise torso
Inhale. Chest forward and up, arms extend. Shoulders back.
✦ Om Hiranya Garbhaya Namaha
20
Parvatasana
Mountain Pose — Inverted V
↓ Exhale, raise buttocks
Exhale. Hips rise — inverted V.
✦ Om Marichaye Namaha
21
Ashwa Sanchalanasana
Equestrian Pose · Left Leg Forward
Left foot steps forward
↑ Inhale, left foot forward
Inhale. Step left foot forward. Lift chest, look forward.
✦ Om Adityaya Namaha
22
Padahastasana
Hand to Foot Pose
↓ Exhale, fold forward
Exhale. Right foot joins left. Fold — palms flat beside the feet.
✦ Om Savitre Namaha
23
Hasta Utthanasana
Raised Arms Pose
↑ Inhale, arms up
Inhale. Rise, arms sweep up overhead.
✦ Om Arkaya Namaha
24
Pranamasana
Prayer Pose · End of Round
↓ Exhale, returnRest · 2–3 breaths between rounds
Exhale. Palms at the heart. Round complete — rest 2–3 breaths before the next.
✦ Om Bhaskaraya Namaha
Step 11 · Standing Asanas

Standing Sequence

Hold each posture for 5 slow breaths unless otherwise noted. Practise both sides before moving to the next asana.

1
Tadasana
Palm Tree Pose
↑ Inhale, raise armsRetain in stretch↓ Exhale, lower arms
Inhale: arms up, fingers interlaced, palms up, rise onto toes. Exhale: lower arms and heels.
2
Tiryaka Tadasana
Swaying Palm Tree Pose
Right side, then Left side
↑ Inhale, arms up↓ Exhale, bend sideways↑ Inhale, return
Inhale: arms up. Exhale: bend sideways from the waist — body faces forward. Inhale to return. Repeat both sides.
3
Ukthanasana
Squat and Rise Pose
↑ Inhale, squat down↓ Exhale, rise up5 rounds
Inhale: squat — back straight, hands on knees. Exhale: rise to standing. 5 rounds.
4
Dwikonasana
Double Angle Pose
↑ Inhale, stand erect↓ Exhale, bend forward, arms up5 breaths
Inhale standing tall, hands clasped behind back. Exhale: fold forward, raising arms away from the back. Inhale to return.
5
Padangusthasana
Big Toe Posture
↑ Inhale, starting position↓ Exhale, bend to toesHold, breathe deeply↑ Inhale to return
Inhale tall. Exhale: fold forward slowly, grasp big toes. Hold and breathe deeply. Inhale to return.
6
Padahastasana
Hand to Foot Pose
↑ Inhale, starting position↓ Exhale, palms to floorHold, breathe slowly
Inhale tall. Exhale: fold forward, palms flat on the floor. Hold and breathe. Inhale to return.
Contra-indications: not for people with serious back complaints, sciatica, heart disease, high blood pressure or abdominal hernia.
7
Trikonasana
Triangle Pose
Right side, then Left side
↑ Inhale, arms wide↓ Exhale, extend sidewaysHold · 5 breaths↑ Inhale to rise
Feet wide, right foot out 90°. Inhale: arms wide. Exhale: extend torso right — right hand to shin, left arm up. Inhale to rise. Repeat left.
8
Utthita Parsvakonasana
Extended Side Angle Pose
Right side, then Left side
↓ Exhale, extend and twistHold · 5 breaths↑ Inhale to rise
From Warrior II, lean right — forearm on thigh or hand outside the foot. Left arm over the ear, one long diagonal line. Exhale into the pose. Inhale to rise.
Modified posture: right elbow on right knee instead of hand to floor; back knee to floor if necessary.
9
Virabhadrasana II
Warrior Pose II
Right side, then Left side
↑ Inhale, arms wideHold · 5 breaths
Feet wide, right foot out 90°, arms at shoulder height. Bend the right knee over the ankle. Gaze over front fingers. Inhale to open. Repeat left.
10
Virabhadrasana I
Warrior Pose I
Right side, then Left side
↑ Inhale, arms upHold · 5 breaths↓ Exhale, release
Feet wide, right foot out 90°, back foot in 45°, hips squared. Inhale: arms up overhead, bend right knee. Tailbone down, chest lifts. Repeat left.
Back heel must stay grounded. If it lifts, widen your stance.
11
Parsvottanasana
Intense Side Stretch Posture
Right side, then Left side
↓ Exhale, fold over front legHold · 5 breaths
Right foot forward, back foot in 45°, elbows clasped behind back. Inhale. Exhale: fold forward over the front leg. Inhale to rise. Repeat left.
Modified posture: hold elbows behind back instead of hands in prayer.
