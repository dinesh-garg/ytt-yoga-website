Prana
yama

YTT Module 03 · Pranayama

The Science of Breath & Vital Energy

Prana — the vital life force. Ayama — to expand, to master. The breath is the bridge between the body and the mind.

scroll

The Practice

Find a comfortable seated position. Keep the spine erect — back, neck, and head in one straight line. Relax the shoulders, forehead, and facial muscles. Gently close the eyes before beginning.

Detailed Reference

The Techniques

✦
Preparation
Setting up for practice
SeatedEyes closedBefore beginning
Sit comfortably in a cross-legged position or on a chair. Spine erect — back, neck, and head in one straight line. Relax the shoulders away from the ears. Soften the forehead and release all tension from the facial muscles. Rest the hands on the knees in Jnana Mudra or Chin Mudra. Gently close the eyes. Take 2–3 natural breaths and simply arrive.
1
Belly Breathing
Diaphragmatic Breath
उदर श्वास
↑ Inhale — belly out↓ Exhale — belly in3–4 rounds
The foundation of all pranayama. Place one hand on the belly. As you inhale, the belly rises — diaphragm descends. As you exhale, the belly falls — diaphragm ascends. Activates the parasympathetic nervous system.
2
Yogic Breathing
Three-Part Breath · Dirga Pranayama
दीर्घ श्वास
↑ Belly → Chest → Clavicle↓ Clavicle → Chest → BellyFull capacity
Fills the lungs in three stages from bottom to top. On the exhale, empty in reverse order. Develops full lung capacity and awareness of the complete breathing cycle. Gentle — do not force.
3
Kapalabhati
Skull Shining Breath
कपालभाति
Forceful exhale — passive inhaleUp to 100 roundsActivating · Cleansing
Exhale sharply from the nostrils — belly pumps in. Inhalation is passive and automatic. Focus entirely on the forceful exhalation. Keep the chest still. Build up gradually. Cleanses the respiratory tract, energises the body, and sharpens mental clarity.
Avoid during menstruation, pregnancy, high blood pressure, heart conditions, or hernia. If dizzy, slow down or stop.
4
Bhastrika
Bellows Breath
भस्त्रिका
↑ Forceful inhale — arms up↓ Forceful exhale — arms down20–40 counts
Both the inhale and exhale are forceful. Arms rise overhead on inhale, lower on exhale. Generates heat, increases oxygen, energises the entire body. Feel the tingling sensation in the nose and lips — a sign of heightened oxygenation.
Go slow with asthma, dizziness, or neck/shoulder pain. Modify by moving arms to the sides instead of overhead.
5
Chandra Bhedana
Left Nostril · Moon Piercing Breath
चन्द्र भेदन
Close right nostrilBreathe only through leftCooling · Calming
Close the right nostril with the thumb. Inhale and exhale only through the left. Activates the right hemisphere of the brain — cooling, receptive, feminine energy. Visualise a full moon at the third eye. Use in summer or when feeling heated or overactive.
6
Surya Bhedana
Right Nostril · Sun Piercing Breath
सूर्य भेदन
Close left nostrilBreathe only through rightWarming · Activating
Close the left nostril with the ring finger. Inhale and exhale only through the right. Activates the left hemisphere — warming, active, masculine energy. Visualise the morning sun at the third eye. Use in winter or when feeling cold, sluggish, or low energy.
7
Nadi Shodhan
Alternate Nostril Breathing · Channel Purification
नाड़ी शोधन
↑ Inhale left → exhale right → inhale right → exhale left3–4 rounds · Balancing
Use the right hand — thumb closes the right nostril, ring finger closes the left. Inhale left → exhale right → inhale right → exhale left = one round. Balances left and right brain, purifies the energy channels (nadis), and brings the mind to stillness. No contraindications — everyone can practice.
8
Ujjayi
Victorious Breath · Ocean Breath
उज्जायी
Throat constrictionWave-like sound5 breaths · Tranquilizing
Slight constriction at the back of the throat creates a soft hissing or oceanic sound on both inhale and exhale. Slight chin tuck. Breathe as if through a hole in the throat. Deepens concentration, calms the nervous system, and bridges the physical practice with meditation. Also used throughout Ashtanga asana practice.
9
Bhramari
Humming Bee Breath
भ्रामरी
Long humming on exhale3–5 roundsDeeply soothing
Shanmukhi Mudra: thumbs close the ears, index fingers on the eyes, middle fingers beside the nostrils, ring and little fingers on the lips. Full inhale, then exhale with a sustained humming sound — like a bee. Feel the vibration in the skull, throat, and chest. Instantly calms anxiety, reduces stress, and prepares the mind for meditation.
Avoid lying down. Those with ear infections can keep the ears open and simply hum.
10
Sheetali · Sheetkari
Cooling Breaths
शीतली · शीतकारी
CoolingSummer practice5–10 rounds
Sheetali: Roll the tongue into a tube. Inhale through the tube, exhale through the nose. Sheetkari: If you cannot roll your tongue — press the tip of the tongue to the upper palate, teeth slightly apart. Inhale through the teeth, exhale through the nose. Both cool the body and mind. Practice in summer or when overheated.
Avoid in cold climates or during winter. Those with low blood pressure should practise with caution.
✦
Closing
Om · Shanti · Sankalpa · Namaste
Om × 1 Shanti × 3 Sankalpa · Namaste
Eyes closed. Sit in silence — observe the colours and stillness behind the eyes. Take a full inhale and chant Om once together. Then: Shanti, Shanti, Shanti. Sit quietly. Visualise your healthiest, most radiant self. Chant your personal Sankalpa three times mentally. Slowly rub the palms, cup them over the eyes. Open gently. Namaste.
