Opening — Greetings & Introduction

🙏
Enter & Settle the Room
Before You Speak
Stand · Smile · Eye contact Let silence land first
  • —Enter calmly — let your own stillness set the tone before you say a word
  • —Make natural, gentle eye contact with the group; smile without performing
  • —Wait until the room quiets on its own — do not rush the beginning
Opening Voice
"Namaste, everyone. Welcome. I'm so grateful to share this space and this time with you today. My name is Dinesh, and today I'll be guiding you through a practice of Anapana Sati — mindfulness of the breath."
📖
Context — What is Anapana Sati?
Brief Introduction · 60 seconds
  • —One of the oldest and most complete meditation techniques — taught by the Buddha in the Ānāpānasati Sutta
  • —Ānā = incoming breath · Apāna = outgoing breath · Sati = mindfulness / awareness
  • —Foundational to Vipassana, Zen, and Theravada traditions
  • —The only tool needed: your own breath — always present, always available
Teacher Voice
"This is one of the oldest and most powerful meditation techniques, taught by the Buddha himself. All you need to bring is your breath — and your full, gentle attention."
02

Mantra — Vakratuṇḍa Mahākāya

🕉
Invocation of Ganesha
Obstacle Remover · Before Practice
Anjali Mudra · Palms at heart Chant × 3
  • —Sit down. Bring palms together at heart center — Añjali Mudrā
  • —Close your eyes briefly; invite the group to do the same
  • —Chant slowly and with intention — 3 repetitions
  • —Hold silence for one full breath after the third repetition before continuing
Teacher Voice
"Let us begin by invoking Ganesha — the remover of obstacles — so that whatever is in the way of our stillness today may be gently cleared."
वक्रतुण्ड महाकाय सूर्यकोटि समप्रभ
निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा
Vakratuṇḍa Mahākāya · Sūryakoṭi Samaprabha
Nirvighnaṃ Kuru Me Deva · Sarva Kāryeṣu Sarvadā
Repeat × 3 · Hold silence after final repetition
Meaning — O Lord with the curved trunk and great form, radiant as a million suns: make all my endeavours free from obstacles, always.
03

Sequence — Anapana Sati Meditation

1
Body Settling — Find Your Seat
Foundation · Arrive in the Body
Sukhasana · or any comfortable seat Spine tall · Shoulders down
  • —Cross-legged or whatever the body allows — comfort over posture perfection here
  • —Spine rises gently, as if a thread draws the crown toward the ceiling
  • —Hands on knees — palms down (grounding) or palms up (receiving)
  • —Shoulders melt away from the ears; jaw softens; face relaxes
  • —Gently close the eyes
Teacher Cue
"Find a comfortable seated position. Sit tall — let your spine rise gently. Shoulders melt away from your ears. Soften your jaw. Soften your face. Gently... close your eyes."
2
Breath Awareness — The Anchor
Core Practice · Return Here Always
↑ Inhale — feel the coolness ↓ Exhale — feel the warmth Focus: nostrils · rim · vibrations
  • —Do not control or change the breath — simply observe it as it is
  • —Bring all attention to the nostrils — right here, at this precise point
  • —Notice the coolness of air flowing in; the warmth flowing out
  • —Feel the subtle vibrations and the gentle touch of air on the rim of the nostrils
  • —Notice the rise and fall of the chest and belly with each breath
  • —This is the anchor — the breath is always here, always with you
Teacher Cue
"Breathe in... and breathe out. Follow the breath as it enters through your nostrils. Feel the coolness as it flows in. The warmth as it flows back out. Can you feel the subtle vibrations — the gentle touch of air at the nostrils? This is your anchor. Stay here."
〰 Pause — 30 to 45 seconds of held silence
3
Working with Thoughts
The Heart of the Practice
⚠ Do NOT fight or suppress thoughts Notice → Watch → Return
  • —The mind will wander — this is not failure, this is what minds do
  • —When a thought arises: memory, plan, worry, sound — do not fight it
  • —Simply notice: "Ah — a thought has come." Name it silently if helpful
  • —Watch it — like a cloud passing through the sky. You are the sky. The thought is the cloud.
  • —Let it pass without following it — then gently return to the breath
  • —Every moment of noticing and returning IS the practice. That moment of return is the meditation.
Teacher Cue
"When a thought comes — do not fight it. Do not judge yourself. Simply notice. 'A thought has come.' Just watch it — like a cloud passing through the sky. You are the sky. Let it pass. And gently... bring your attention back. Back to the nostrils. Back to the breath."
〰 Pause — 45 to 60 seconds of held silence
Remind students: the moment of noticing distraction and returning is not a failure — it is the practice itself. Repeat this teaching if needed.
4
Body Scan — Head to Toe
Vipassanā Element · Sensation Awareness
Crown → Forehead → Face → Neck Chest → Belly → Hips → Feet Observe only · do not change
  • —Move awareness slowly — do not rush through any region
  • —At each area: simply observe. Warmth, tingling, pressure, heaviness, or nothing — all are valid
  • —Do not try to relax or change anything — only observe what is already there
  • —Entire body: present, alive, breathing
Teacher Cue — Full Body Scan (speak slowly)
"Begin at the crown of your head... observe. Move down to the forehead... the temples... the eyes... the cheeks... the jaw. The neck and throat. The shoulders... and down through both arms... the elbows... the wrists... the fingertips. Feel whatever is there — warmth, heaviness, pulsing. Now the chest — feel it rise with every inhale, fall with every exhale. The upper back... mid-back... the belly. Move down to the hips... the thighs... the knees. The calves... the ankles... the feet... and the very tips of your toes. Your entire body — present. Alive. Breathing."
〰 Pause — 30 seconds · then return to breath at nostrils
Return Cue
"Now bring the awareness back to the breath once more. Nostrils. Vibrations. In... and out. In... and out."
〰 Final hold — 30 to 45 seconds of silence
04

Savasana — Complete Rest

☽
Transition to Corpse Pose
Śavāsana · शवासन
Slowly lower to the back Legs extended · Palms up Hold — 2 to 3 minutes minimum
  • —Guide the transition slowly and quietly — no abrupt instructions
  • —Legs extend fully; feet fall naturally outward
  • —Arms rest by the sides — palms facing upward: open, receiving
  • —The floor holds the body completely — no effort required
  • —Savasana is not sleep — it is conscious, effortless awareness
Teacher Cue
"Now, very gently, begin to let go of any effort at all. Slowly lower yourself down onto your back. Legs extend fully. Arms rest by your sides — palms facing upward, open, receiving. Let the floor hold you completely. You don't need to do anything. You have nowhere to be but here. Let the body be heavy. Let the breath be natural. Let the mind rest."
〰 2 to 3 minutes of deep silence — do not speak
This is the most important part of the practice. Do not shorten it. The integration of the meditation happens here.
↑
Coming Back Up
Re-emergence · Slow & Intentional
↑ Deepen the breath Fingers → Toes → Right side → Sit up
  • —Begin by deepening the breath — no sudden cues
  • —Invite gentle movement of fingers, then toes
  • —Roll to the right side — use the arm as a pillow; stay here for one full breath
  • —Slowly press up to seated — no rush, no force
  • —Wait in silence until the entire group has returned and settled
Teacher Cue
"Slowly begin to bring your awareness back to the room. Start to deepen the breath — feel the air filling your lungs. Gently wiggle your fingers... your toes. Roll to your right side, using your arm as a pillow. Stay here for a breath. And slowly, gently — press yourself up to a comfortable seated position. Take your time."
05

Closing — Gratitude & Quote

Anjali Mudra — Return to Stillness
Closing Gesture
  • —Bring palms together at the heart — Añjali Mudrā
  • —Bow slightly; guide the group to mirror you
  • —Pause here — feel the warmth of the palms at the heart center
Teacher Voice
"Bring your hands to your heart. Feel the warmth there. Before we close, I'd like to leave you with a few words."
✦
