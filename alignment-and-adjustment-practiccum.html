01

About the Pose

✦
What is it?
  • —Intermediate kneeling backbend — arched spine resembles a camel's hump, so the name
  • —Introduced through the Iyengar Yoga lineage; not in classical Hatha texts
  • —Opens the entire front body — chest, abdomen, hip flexors, quadriceps
  • —Activates Heart Chakra and also Throat Chakra in full expression
💪
Strengthens
  • —Spine
  • —Thighs
  • —Core
  • —Glutes
🤸
Stretches
  • —Chest
  • —Quadriceps
  • —Hamstrings
  • —Shoulders
  • —Hips
  • —Abdomen
  • —Neck
02

Benefits

✦
  • —Restores natural spinal curves
  • —Expands chest and increases lung capacity; good for asthma and shallow breathing
  • —Relieves lower back pain
  • —Stimulates thyroid glands; improves energy and elimination
  • —Emotional release and openness
03

Contraindications

⚠
  • —Spinal or knee injuries
  • —High or low blood pressure
  • —Vertigo or epilepsy
  • —Recent stomach surgery
  • —Pregnancy
04

Teaching the Asana

1
Start in Vajrasana
Foundation
Pre-pose · settle the breath
  • —Sit on the heels — toes together, heels apart
  • —Knees hip-width apart, spine long
  • —Take two or three natural breaths to arrive
  • —Offer a blanket between thighs and calves if knees are uncomfortable
Teacher Cue
"Come to Vajrasana — sit on your heels. Toes together, heels apart, knees hip-width. Close your eyes and let the breath settle."
2
Rise onto the Knees
Setup
↑ Inhale — rise up
  • —Inhale and rise to stand on the knees
  • —Knees hip-width apart, thighs vertical
  • —Press the center of the ankle and shin bone firmly into the mat
  • —Large gap at the ankle? Place a rolled blanket underneath
Teacher Cue
"Inhale — rise onto your knees. Thighs vertical. Press the center of your ankle and shin into the ground. Gap at the ankle? Use a rolled blanket here."
3
Hands to Waist — Sacrum Anchor
Foundation
Both hands on lower back
  • —Both hands to the back of the waist
  • —Fingers wrap around the sides
  • —Tips of thumbs close to the sacrum — the tailbone
  • —Hands are active — they support the lower back throughout
Teacher Cue
"Both hands to the back of your waist. Fingers to the sides, thumbs toward the tailbone. This is your anchor."
4
Open the Chest — Shoulder Action
Key Alignment
↑ Inhale — lift the inner armpits
  • —Lift the inner armpits upward
  • —Roll shoulders back and down
  • —Draw elbows toward each other behind you
  • —Collar bones widen, sternum lifts
  • —This must happen before leaning back — not after
Teacher Cue
"Lift your inner armpits — feel the chest open. Shoulders back, elbows toward each other. Sternum lifts. Open the chest here, before we lean back."
5
Rise from the Thighs — Most Critical
⚑ Most Common Error
↑ Inhale — engage both thighs ⚠ Do NOT press knees into the floor
  • —Common error: pressing the knees hard down to push hips up — shifts all weight into the knee joint
  • —Correct: engage the front (quadriceps) and back (hamstrings) of the thighs and rise from there
  • —Knees stay relaxed on the mat — not actively pressing down
  • —Power comes from the thighs, not the floor
Teacher Cue
"Engage the front and back of your thighs — quadriceps, hamstrings. Rise from your thighs. Do not press the knees into the floor. Can you feel the difference?"
Demonstrate the wrong way first, then the right way — let students feel the contrast. This is the pedagogical heart of the pose.
6
Pelvis Forward — Begin the Backbend
Core Alignment
↑ Inhale — lift chest ↓ Exhale — lean back slowly Hips forward · in line with knees
  • —Move the pelvis forward — hip points in line with the knees
  • —Do not drop the hips backward as you lean
  • —Two opposing vectors: hips forward, chest back
  • —Inhale — lift chest and sternum first; exhale — slowly lean back
  • —Collapsing the chest first causes lumbar compression — always lift first, then lean
Teacher Cue
"Hips forward, in line with the knees. Inhale — lift the chest. Exhale — lean back slowly. Chest keeps lifting. Hips keep moving forward. Two directions at once."
7
Gaze — Where to Look
Neck & Head
Look forward first → then gently upward
  • —Ideal dṛṣṭi in full expression: upward (ūrdhva)
  • —For beginners — look forward first
  • —Looking straight up too early crunches the cervical spine — causes dizziness and compression
  • —Keep the back of the neck long; let the gaze travel upward gradually
Teacher Cue
"Look forward for now — keep the back of the neck long. When ready, let the gaze travel gently upward. Do not drop the head. Feeling dizzy? Chin slowly to the chest."
8
Full Expression — Hands to Heels
Final Pose
↑ Inhale — one last chest lift Right hand → right heel · Left hand → left heel Hold 5–8 breaths
  • —Right hand to right heel, left hand to left heel
  • —Pelvis continues forward, chest continues lifting
  • —Full stretch across the front body — sternum, ribcage, abdomen, hip flexors, quads
  • —Thigh muscles stay engaged; rib cage draws softly in to protect the lower back
  • —Can't reach the heels? Hands at waist is a complete pose — never collapse just to reach
Teacher Cue
"If you're ready — right hand to the right heel, left to the left. Pelvis forward. Chest lifts. Breathe here. Five breaths."
9
Exit & Counter-Pose
Non-Negotiable
↑ Inhale — lift chest ↓ Exhale — hands to waist ↓ Exhale — lower hips → Bālāsana · 8–10 breaths
  • —Inhale — lift the chest one last time
  • —Exhale — release both hands to the waist simultaneously, with control
  • —Slowly lower hips back to Vajrasana
  • —Fold into Bālāsana (Child's Pose) — arms extended, forehead to mat
  • —Hold 8–10 breaths — spine decompresses, nervous system settles
Teacher Cue
"Inhale — lift the chest one last time. Exhale — both hands to the waist. Slowly lower your hips. Fold into Child's Pose. Arms long, forehead down. Breathe here."
Bālāsana after Ustrasana is not optional — it is the completion of the practice.
05

The Adjustment

①
Confirm the Student is Settled
  • —Student must be stable — both hands on heels, pelvis forward, chest open
  • —Confirm they are breathing steadily before approaching
Teacher Cue
"Right hand to your right foot, left hand to your left foot. Good. Stay there — chest lifting."
②
Sit in Front, Facing the Student
  • —Sit directly in front, facing them
  • —Place the soles of your feet against the pelvis — the hip points (ASIS)
  • —Contact only — no force, no pushing
  • —Narrate this clearly to the whole class as you do it
Teacher Cue — To the Class
"I am sitting in front of her. I am not going to push. I am simply placing my feet against the pelvis — as a reference point for her to press into."
③
Cue the Active Press — Student Does the Work
Student presses pelvis into teacher's feet Teacher does NOT push back
  • —Ask the student to actively press the pelvis forward into your feet
  • —Your feet are a wall to press against — not a tool to push with
  • —Simultaneously cue: press pelvis forward and lift chest at the same time
  • —No engagement felt? Say directly: "You are not pressing. Press more."
  • —Still no contact? "Okay, come back" — reset and try again
Teacher Cue
"Press your pelvis into my feet — actively press forward. At the same time — lift your chest. Press... and chest up. More. Yes — feel that?"
④
Close the Adjustment — Teach the Class
  • —Address the class on what they just observed
  • —Depth comes from active forward movement of the pelvis — not from leaning further back
  • —No pelvis engagement = forced backbend = lumbar compression
  • —Pelvis forward + chest up = intelligent backbend = space and extension
Closing Teacher Voice
"Did you see what happened? The depth came not from going further back — but from pressing the pelvis forward while the chest lifted. Two directions at once. That is the intelligence of this pose."
