Applied Anatomy for Yoga Teachers

The body is a map. Knowing its terrain is what separates a good teacher from a safe one.

About This Module

Applied anatomy for yoga is the study of the human body through the lens of movement, posture, and breath. For a yoga teacher, understanding anatomy is not optional — it is the responsibility that makes practice safe, intelligent, and deeply transformative.

This module covers the skeletal system and joint mechanics, the major muscle groups and their roles in asana, the nervous system and its relationship to stress and relaxation, and the anatomy of breathing — the diaphragm, intercostals, and the mechanics of pranayama.

We examine common injuries, contraindications for specific postures, and how to intelligently modify practice for different bodies, conditions, and life stages.
